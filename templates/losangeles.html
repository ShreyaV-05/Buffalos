Italian

    

    
Eataly

    
    
    This three story building filled with many
    italian restaurants and stores is just the
    place to go for Italy in America. Restaurants
    filled with the aroma of pasta,pizza,and paninis
    are complimented with shops that sell wines,
    cheeses, meats, and olive oil. Offering both
    pickup and delivery (unfortunately no outdoor
    seating for a majority),you can binge your
    favorite foods from the comfort of your home.



    Eataly Website
    
    

    

    
Palermo

    
    
    This italian restaurant has
    a large variety of appetizers
    varying from garlic bread to
    fried calamari. Their main courses
    allow vegetarians, pescatarians,
    and the rest to eat together. They
    have special dishes such as Pizza
    Alla Siciliana Special and
    Cannelloni that are sure to leave
    you craving more.

{% block hotspot2 %}


    
Asian

    

    
Badmaash- An Indian Cuisine

    
    
    While this cousine directly translates to
    troublemaker, their food is anything but.
    Located near Beverly Hills their menu offers
    a wide variety of both vegetarian dishes such
    as Daal Makhani and meats such as Murgh Makhani
    with a side of Naan (Indian bread) you’ll
    be licking your plates clean. While seating
    at the restaurant is currently closed their
    online service is running so you can order food
    from the comfort of your home.
    Badmaash Website
        

    
    

    
Yang Chow- A Chinese Cuisine

        
    
    Opened in 1977, located in the heart of Chinatown.
    Yang Chow is known for its Slippery Shrimp dish
    (even cited in a LA Times article!). With appetizers
    such as steamed dumpling and spring rolls to begin
    your meal, you transition into an assortment of soup,
    noodles, specialities, and combo deals. So if you want
    to simply have a night of takeout or driving by Chinatown,
    Yang Chow is your best bet!
    Yang Chow Website

Mexican

    

    
Tlayuda- An Oaxacan Cuisine

    
    
    This Oaxacan restaurant focuses their
    dishes around Mexican staples such as corns,
    beans, and peppers. Offering both non-vegetarian
    and vegetarian options along with take-out options,
    Tlayuda’s family run business aims to please. Drive
    on by and have one of their speciality dishes,
    such as their Torta Oaxaquena.




    Tlayuda Website
    
    

    

    
The Mexican Village

    
    
    This quaint restaurant offers
    not only delicious food but also
    late night dancing. Their menu
    does not disappoint either with
    Queso Fundido, Mexican Village
    Botana, or the ever amazing Carne
    Asada Fries all for very low prices.
    This is the perfect spot to relax,
    let loose, and have good food.



    The Mexican Village Website
